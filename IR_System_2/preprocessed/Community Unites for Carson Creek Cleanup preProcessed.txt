a dedicated crew of 150 volunteers braved light drizzle on saturday to clean up carson creek removing nearly nine tons of debris in just eight hours a job well done remarked alan specter the event director were scheduled to return here in three years but we hope there wont be as much garbage next time

the assortment of garbage collected was astonishing ranging from cans and bottles to bicycles car tires auto batteries and even sofas there were also shopping carts clothing bowling balls plastic bags dolls baby carriages tv antennas and portable radios one young volunteer even unearthed a golf bag complete with a full set of clubs

two community groupsthe cub scouts and boy scoutsalong with environmental organizations like save the bay and watch the whales played a significant role in the cleanup effort concerned retirees and volunteers from local police and fire departments joined in making the event a true community effort each volunteer was provided with boots gloves and rain gear ready to tackle the twomile stretch of streambed over 500 large yellow trash bags were filled with the collected debris which was then transported by trucks to the landfill

while no valuable treasures were discovered one fiveyearold boy found a shiny earring that he believed could be worth a million dollars he excitedly announced his plan to sell it promising to donate half of the proceeds to watch the whales and use the other half to buy a triplescoop ice cream cone every day for the rest of his life
